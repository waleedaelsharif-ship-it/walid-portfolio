Pharmacy KPI Operating System

From raw pharmacy activity to executive control.

Context

Pharmacy leadership operated with fragmented reports. Metrics existed, decisions did not. Teams optimized isolated numbers, creating stockouts, service delays, and compliance risk.

Decision Risk

  • Promoting speed without safety increased dispensing errors.
  • Chasing sales without inventory logic amplified stockouts.
  • Compliance treated as audits, not a living control system.

Objective

Design a single KPI operating system that aligns daily pharmacist behavior with outcomes, balancing service, safety, inventory, and profitability while enabling weekly executive decisions.

Data Used

  • Sales, margin, transactions, items per basket
  • Inventory levels, OOS logs, expiry data
  • Dispensing time, queue duration
  • Medication error logs
  • Compliance checkpoints (CIBAHI, JCI)

KPI Architecture

Level 1 (Outcome / Lagging): Revenue, Gross Margin, Patient Satisfaction (NPS proxy), Compliance Score

Level 2 (Control / Leading): Stockout Rate, Average Waiting Time, Dispensing Error Rate, Conversion Rate, Average Basket Value

Level 3 (Behavioral): Service Recommendation Rate, Accuracy per Pharmacist, Stock Refill Discipline, Queue Handling Compliance

Each KPI had an owner, thresholds (Green/Amber/Red), and a mandatory action when breached.

Decisions Taken

  • Reset reorder points using demand logic, not intuition.
  • Rebalanced pharmacist workload based on queue analytics.
  • Stopped promotion-driven stock depletion.
  • Embedded compliance checks into daily operations.

Results

  • Stockouts ↓ 35%
  • Waiting time ↓ 20%
  • Dispensing errors ↓ 30%
  • 100% CIBAHI & JCI compliance

What the Decision-Maker Gained

A governed system where performance became predictable, risk surfaced early, and pharmacist behavior aligned with strategy.
